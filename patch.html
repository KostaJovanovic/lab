Round Trip

2.09

Long Play

7 June 2026, 16:15

  • Raw camera and dash-cam video opens properly now - even multi-gigabyte files.
  • FixRaw H.264 / H.265 clips actually play. The in-browser step that wraps a bare .h264 / .265 stream into a playable MP4 was failing to start, so these clips wouldn’t open at all. It now loads reliably and they play in the normal player - which also restored the audio extraction and frame capture that rely on it.
  • NewVery large raw streams play in parts. A stream too big to convert in one piece - the multi-GB recordings dash-cams and capture cards write - is now split at its keyframes into parts you step through one at a time, with a loading bar while each part prepares.
  • NewEach part gets the full treatment - frame-by-frame navigation with editable timecode, send a frame to photo analysis, grab it as a PNG, the codec and profile readout, resolution and aspect ratio, on-demand SHA-256, and opt-in scene detection for the part you’re watching.

2.05

Green Light

6 June 2026, 17:50

  • FixOrdinary videos no longer get turned away. A bug in how Analyser read a video’s colour format could misread a perfectly normal 8-bit H.264 / HEVC file as exotic 4:2:2 or 12-bit footage and show the “can’t play this - use VLC” banner instead of playing it. It now reads the format reliably, so playable files play.
  • FixAVI clips (the Motion-JPEG kind older cameras record, like Canon MVI_*.AVI) now reliably show their frames and extracted audio again, rather than the unplayable banner or a black player - Analyser decodes them itself instead of waiting on the browser.
  • NewDropping a video or audio file now scrolls straight to its section, so the player and analysis are in front of you without a scroll.
  • NewPDF page previews moved to the top of the results and lost their duplicate buttons: the Open-in-browser buttons are gone, and on a phone or tablet each preview’s Analyse / OCR / PNG buttons are now always visible and tappable instead of hidden behind a hover you can’t do on touch.

2.03

Solid State

6 June 2026, 17:05

  • Two new viewers - a real 3D model viewer and an editing-timeline viewer - plus frame-accurate tools for video.
  • NewThe STL viewer is now a full 3D model viewer. It opens OBJ, PLY, OFF, 3MF and AMF as well, and tessellates STEP, IGES and BREP CAD solids with OpenCASCADE so you can orbit them in the browser. STEP files also name the CAD system, version and AP203/214/242 protocol they came from, and 3MF/AMF let you inspect each model and assembly on the build plate one at a time.
  • NewA new editing-timeline viewer draws the sequences exported from Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, DaVinci Resolve and Avid - EDL (CMX3600), FCPXML and OpenTimelineIO (OTIO) - as visual tracks of clip blocks placed by timecode, with durations, clip names and frame rate.
  • NewThe video player gains frame-accurate controls: an editable timecode you click to jump to an exact hour : minute : second : frame, step a single frame back or forward, send the current frame to the photo analysis, or grab it as a PNG - plus a contact sheet of thumbnails you can open full-size.
  • FixRAW photo previews come out more reliably. On some cameras - the Sony FX30 among them - the embedded-preview scan could lock onto a chunk of raw sensor noise instead of the real JPEG; it now insists on a genuine image marker, so the correct preview shows.
  • FixThe photo aspect-ratio readout now shows the nearest standard ratio with its own decimal beside the exact one, so the two read side by side - e.g. 258:145 (1.7793) ≈ 16:9 (1.7778).

Wide Angle

6 June 2026, 05:10

  • FixLarge videos load faster - the first frame is grabbed only when you ask for it, and the checksum is skipped by default on big files. The spectrogram now opens on its logarithmic frequency axis, closer to how you hear.

Dependencies
Metadata
exifr. EXIF · IPTC · XMP · ICC.
OCR
tesseract.js. On-device text recognition with language picker.
QR codes
jsQR. Scans for QR codes in photos. Loaded only when needed.
Map
Leaflet with OpenStreetMap tiles. Loaded only if the photo has GPS.
HEIC
heic2any. Converts HEIC/HEIF to JPEG in the browser.
RAW
magick-wasm. ImageMagick compiled to WASM. Converts ARW, CR2, CR3, NEF, and other RAW formats. ~15 MB, loaded only when needed.
Audio
Native Web Audio API plus a hand-written radix-2 FFT for spectrogram and analysis. BPM read from ID3/MP4 tags when available, estimated via onset detection otherwise.
Video
HTML5 video for playback. Frame rate parsed from MP4 container metadata with FFmpeg fallback. Automatic scene detection with confidence scoring. iOS visible-player fallback.
FFmpeg
ffmpeg.wasm. Audio extraction and frame rate detection for formats the browser can't decode natively. The ~31 MB core is fetched from a CDN the first time it's needed (then cached for offline use).
PDF
pdf.js by Mozilla. Renders PDF pages, extracts text, and supports OCR scanning of image-only pages via Tesseract. Loaded only for PDF files.
Archives
fflate. Fast ZIP decompression in the browser.
File ID
Built-in identification for 200+ file formats. Adobe, SolidWorks, Fusion 360, AutoCAD, Blender, Office, 3D mesh, executables, fonts, G-Code, log files, and more. Extracts version, author, and metadata from headers, ZIP manifests, ID3 tags, and gzipped XML projects. DOCX documents render with formatted text. AI-generation markers detected in photo metadata.
Install
Add to home screen for an offline PWA. Service worker caches the app shell. Use the buttons above to pre-cache heavy dependencies.
